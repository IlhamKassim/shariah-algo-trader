# Gap and Go as an unconstrained benchmark strategy

The Day Trader runs a Gap and Go strategy in a separate Alpaca paper account, on a 45-stock unrestricted watchlist, purely to serve as a benchmark against the Shariah bot's Portfolio on the Compare page. It was chosen because it is close to the maximum possible contrast with the Shariah bot on every axis: universe (unrestricted, including leveraged ETFs, crypto-adjacent names, and banks explicitly excluded from the Eligible Universe) versus screened; holding period (intraday only, flat by EOD liquidation, no overnight exposure) versus month-long Portfolio holds; and signal (a fast overnight gap + first-minute RVOL price/volume trigger) versus the slow, fundamentals-driven Factor Score. Both strategies are fully systematic and rules-based, so the comparison isolates the effect of Shariah compliance and factor discipline rather than comparing two similar systematic approaches or introducing discretionary judgment on either side.

The alternative was to rely solely on the SPUS benchmark already shown on the Overview performance chart. SPUS answers "did the Portfolio beat a passive index within its own compliant universe" but says nothing about what is given up by choosing compliance and diversification over unconstrained, momentum-seeking trading — the Day Trader fills that gap as a second, active-style benchmark. Reusing the existing architecture (APScheduler, an Alpaca paper account, the same FastAPI dashboard) to run a structurally opposite strategy was also low additional engineering cost, and closing all positions every day before EOD gives a clean daily P&L series that's directly comparable to the Portfolio's equity curve without normalising for holding-period differences.
