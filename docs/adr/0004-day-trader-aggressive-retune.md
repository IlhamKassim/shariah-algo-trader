# Retuning the Day Trader toward higher risk / higher return

The Day Trader's configuration was retuned to deliberately maximise risk and return within the existing architecture: `DAY_GAP_PCT` 3%→4.5% and `DAY_RVOL_THRESHOLD` 1.5×→2.5× (a considered middle ground — literal professional practice treats 5×+ RVOL as the real-mover signal, but this watchlist is large/mid-cap rather than true low-float, and only 2 of 135 names cleared even the old 1.5× bar on a typical morning, so 5× would starve the strategy of trades most days); `DAY_POSITION_SIZE_PCT` 10%→20% and `DAY_PROFIT_TARGET_MULT` 2.0→3.0 to size up and let winners run further; `DAY_STOP_LOSS_PCT` 2%→5% and `DAY_TRAILING_STOP_PCT` 1.5%→3% to give the more volatile curated watchlist room to move instead of getting stopped out by ordinary noise. The watchlist itself (`day_trader/data/watchlist.py`) was cut from 135 to ~76 tickers, dropping mega-cap anchors, banks, index/broad-sector ETFs, industrials, energy majors, and big pharma in favour of names that actually produce large catalyst-driven gaps: leveraged ETFs, crypto-adjacent, high-growth SaaS, semiconductors, EV, binary-catalyst biotech, and momentum/growth names. `DAY_MAX_LOSS_PCT` (the circuit breaker) was deliberately kept, only loosened slightly (5%→7%) to account for the wider per-trade stops now costing more equity per stop-out — not removed, since a full account blowup on day one would leave nothing left to compare against the Shariah bot going forward.

The alternative was true low-float scanning (sub-20M share float, the professional "Gap and Go" archetype) driven by pre-market volume and float data, which is what actually produces the largest single-day moves. That requires a new external data provider (Finnhub or Polygon.io) since Alpaca doesn't expose float or reliable pre-market volume, plus replacing the static watchlist with a daily dynamic scan across the full market. Both are meaningful infrastructure investments — a new paid dependency and a new discovery mechanism — for what is explicitly a benchmark strategy, not the project's core deliverable, and illiquid low-float names also fill very differently in Alpaca's paper simulation than they would live, so the resulting equity curve would overstate what's achievable. This tier — retuning existing knobs and curating the existing watchlist toward already-available high-beta names — was chosen as the ceiling on risk/return achievable without that investment; revisit if the benchmark's purpose ever expands beyond illustrating contrast against the Shariah bot.
